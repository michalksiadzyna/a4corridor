Transaction Process

Purchasing land in Poland follows a clear, regulated process. All transactions require notarial form and are recorded in the public land registry.

StepDescriptionTimeline
1. Due diligenceBuyer reviews: land registry (księga wieczysta), MPZP zoning extract, soil classification, utility conditions, tax clearance.1–2 weeks
2. Preliminary agreementOptional: notarized preliminary agreement (umowa przedwstępna) with deposit (zadatek, typically 10%). Locks in price and terms.1 day
3. Final notarial deedSigned before a Polish notary (akt notarialny). Transfers ownership immediately. Both parties present or represented by power of attorney.1 day
4. Land registry updateNotary files for ownership change in księga wieczysta. Court processes within 1–3 months.1–3 months
5. Tax obligationsBuyer pays 2% PCC (tax on civil transactions) unless VAT applies. Seller handles any capital gains obligations.At signing

For EU/EEA/US buyers: No special permits required. The transaction process is identical to a domestic purchase. Non-EU buyers purchasing agricultural land face UKUR restrictions — but these plots are exempt due to P/UO.4 zoning.
